Wisconsin State Supreme Court Elections:

County-Level Trends in Electoral Margin (2016-2026)

For decades, judicial elections in the United States seemed isolated from the intense polarization of the political branches. An evolving, increasingly partisan, and nationalized environment, however, have jointly manifested in record-setting outside spending and an abandonment of conventional impartiality in judicial campaigns.

The seven (7) justices of the Wisconsin State Supreme Court are directly elected to ten (10) year terms in staggered off-cycle April elections. From 2016-2026, there were eight (8) such elections, seven (7) of which were contested, with critical implications for the court majority.
